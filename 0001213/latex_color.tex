\centerline{{\bf{Abstract}}}
\vspace{5mm}

We study properties of (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) nonthreshold bound states
(\myHighlight{$2 \le p \le 6 $}\coordHE{}) in the dual gravity description. These bound
states can be viewed as D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with a nonzero NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field of rank
two. We find that in the decoupling limit, the thermodynamics of the \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{}
coincident D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is the same not only as that of \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{}
coincident D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes without \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field, but also as that of the \myHighlight{$N_{p-2}$}\coordHE{}
coincident D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes with two smeared coordinates and no \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field,
for \myHighlight{$N_{p-2}/N_p= \tilde{V}_2/[(2\pi)^2 \tilde{b}]$}\coordHE{} with \myHighlight{$\tilde{V}_2$}\coordHE{} being
the area of the two smeared directions and \myHighlight{$\tilde{b}$}\coordHE{} a noncommutativity
parameter. We also obtain the same relation from the thermodynamics and
dynamics by probe methods. This suggests that the noncommutative super
Yang-Mills with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p+1$}\coordHE{}) dimensions is equivalent to
an ordinary one with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(\infty)$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p-1$}\coordHE{}) dimensions in the
limit \myHighlight{$\tilde{V}_2 \to \infty$}\coordHE{}. We also find that the free energy
of a D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field in the background of D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with
\myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field coincides with that of a D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe in the background of
D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes without \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field.

%%========================section 1 =============================
\sect{Introduction}

Recently there has been much interest in studying the properties of
the (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states and their consequences (see for
example~\cite{Hashimoto1}-\cite{Lu} and references therein).
Such bound states can be viewed as D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with a nonzero (rank two)
Neveu-Schwarz (NS) \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field. At present, it is known that the worldvolume
coordinates will become noncommutative if a D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane carries a
nonvanishing NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field on its worldvolume~\cite{Li}-\cite{Seiberg},
and the field theories on the worldvolume of such D-branes are called
noncommutative field theories in order to distinguish the ordinary field
theories on the worldvolume of D-branes without NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field. The gauge
theories on the noncommutative spacetimes can naturally be realized in
string theories. According to the Maldacena
conjecture~\cite{Mald2}-\cite{Itzhaki}, the string theories can be used to
study the large \myHighlight{$N$}\coordHE{} noncommutative field theories in the strong 't Hooft
coupling limit.

On the basis of consideration of planar diagrams, Bigatti and Susskind
\cite{BS} argued that the large \myHighlight{$N$}\coordHE{} noncommutative and ordinary gauge field
theories are equivalent in the weak coupling limit and noncommutative
effects can be seen only in the nonplanar diagrams. Explicit perturbative
calculations~\cite{Arcioni} render evidence to this assertion. On the
supergravity side, it has also been found that the thermodynamics of
near-extremal D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with a nonvanishing NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field coincides exactly
with that of the corresponding D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes without \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{}
field~\cite{Mald1,Ali,Bar,CO,Harmark}, which means that in the large \myHighlight{$N$}\coordHE{}
and strong coupling limit, the number of the degrees of freedom of
the noncommutative gauge fields remains unchanged despite the
noncommutativity of space.

More recently, Lu and Roy~\cite{Lu} have found that in the system of
(D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states (\myHighlight{$2 \le p \le 6$}\coordHE{}), the noncommutative effects
of gauge fields are actually due to the presence of {\it infinitely many}
D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes which play the dominant role over the D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes in the
large \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field limit. The D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with a constant \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field represent
dynamically the system of infinitely many D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes with two smeared
transverse coordinates (additional isometrics) and no \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field in the
decoupling limit. With this observation, Lu and Roy further argued that
there is an equivalence between the noncommutative super Yang-Mills theory
in \myHighlight{$(p+1)$}\coordHE{} dimensions and an ordinary one with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(\infty)$}\coordHE{} in
\myHighlight{$(p-1)$}\coordHE{} dimensions. For related discussions, see
also~\cite{T,Ishibashi1,Ishibashi2,CS,Corn,Kato}.

In the present paper, we would like to discuss this equivalence from the
viewpoint of the thermodynamics of the (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states
in the dual gravity description with two dimensions compactified on a torus.
The case discussed by Lu and Roy~\cite{Lu} corresponds to the infinite
volume limit of the torus, and we would like to clarify some subtle questions
in this analysis.

In the next section, we study the black (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) configuration and
some of its basic thermodynamic properties. As mentioned above, it has been
noticed that the thermodynamics of the black D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is
the same as that of D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes without \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field. We show here that the
thermodynamics of the D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with nonzero \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is also completely
the same as that of D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes with two smeared coordinates and zero
\myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field. We obtain a relation [eq.~(\ref{number}) below] between
the numbers of D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field and the D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes without
\myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field when this equivalence is valid. Since the worldvolume theory of
D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is the noncommutative super Yang-Mills with gauge
group \myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p+1$}\coordHE{}) dimensions with two dimensions compactified on
a torus and that of D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes with two smeared coordinates and zero \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{}
field is an ordinary super Yang-Mills with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(N_{p-2})$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p+1$}\coordHE{})
dimensions on the dual torus, as we will see, this implies that these
theories are equivalent in the large \myHighlight{$N$}\coordHE{} limit. When the volume of the torus
is sent to infinity, the latter theory reduces to \myHighlight{$U(\infty)$}\coordHE{} gauge theory
in (\myHighlight{$p-1$}\coordHE{}) dimensions for fixed \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{}, in agreement with \cite{Lu}.
This is also in accordance with the proposal that D2-brane is a condensate
of D0-branes~\cite{T,Ishibashi1,Ishibashi2}. Furthermore, we give the
relation of Yang-Mills coupling constants between the theories in different
dimensions.

Investigating the interactions between a probe and a source is a useful
method to see some of the properties of the source. For instance, a scalar
field has been used~\cite{Kaya,Myung1,Myung2} as a probe to find out the
noncommutative effects on the absorption by the (D1, D3) bound state.
In section 3, we study the descriptions of the (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound
state in terms of the D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field and in terms of the
D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes without \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field by analyzing the thermodynamics of two
probes, one of which is a bound state of D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}- and D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes and the
other is a D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-brane. In the decoupling limit, we find a relation
(\ref{3e18}) similar to (\ref{number}). In section 4, we further
reveal the equivalence of the descriptions by examining the dynamics of
the probes in the bound state backgrounds. We find that non-extremal
D-branes can be located at the horizon from the viewpoint of probes.
We summarize our results in section 5.

strong coupling limit~\cite{Mald1,Ali,Bar,Harmark}.

however, the theory reduces to a (\myHighlight{$p-1$}\coordHE{})-dimensional ordinary super Yang-Mills
theory. Therefore we again arrive at the conclusion that the
(\myHighlight{$p+1$}\coordHE{})-dimensional noncommutative gauge field is equivalent to an ordinary
gauge field with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(\infty)$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p-1$}\coordHE{}) dimensions for
\myHighlight{$\tilde{V}_2 \to \infty$}\coordHE{}, from the point of view of dual gravity description.

Next let us address another evidence to render support of the above
equivalence from the viewpoint of thermodynamics. We find that the
thermodynamics of decoupling limit solution (\ref{dp2}) of the D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes
is completely the same as those in (\ref{thermo}). The worldvolume theory of
the (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states (\ref{2e1}) (or \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{} coinciding D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes
with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field) is a noncommutative gauge field theory with gauge group
\myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} in \myHighlight{$(p+1)$}\coordHE{} dimensions with two dimensions compactified on a torus,
while the worldvolume theory is an ordinary gauge field theory with the
same gauge group \myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} if the NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is absent. The above
equivalence of the descriptions of the (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states
implies that the bound states can also be described by ordinary gauge field
theories in \myHighlight{$(p+1)$}\coordHE{} dimensions. Moreover, the equivalence is valid also
between the \myHighlight{$(p+1)$}\coordHE{}-dimensional noncommutative \myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} theory and the
\myHighlight{$(p-1)$}\coordHE{}-dimensional \myHighlight{$U(\infty)$}\coordHE{} ordinary theory in the limit \myHighlight{${\tilde V}_2
\to \infty$}\coordHE{} for the reason described above. For finite volume, the
equivalence is between the \myHighlight{$(p+1)$}\coordHE{}-dimensional noncommutative \myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} gauge
field and a (twisted) ordinary \myHighlight{$U(N_{p-2})$}\coordHE{} gauge field with the
relation~(\ref{number}), the latter living on a dual torus. In this case,
the Yang-Mills coupling constant is
\begin{equation}\coord{}\boxEquation{
\label{const2}
g^2_{\rm YM}=\frac{(2\pi)^p \tilde{g}\tilde{b}^2}{\tilde{V}_2},
}{
g^2_{\rm YM}=\frac{(2\pi)^p \tilde{g}\tilde{b}^2}{\tilde{V}_2},
}{ecuacion}\coordE{}\end{equation}
for the \myHighlight{$(p+1)$}\coordHE{}-dimensional ordinary gauge field theory. As a self-consistency
check, one may find that the coupling constant (\ref{const1}) can also be
obtained from (\ref{const2}) after a trivial dimensional reduction. In the
following sections we will further discuss the equivalence of the descriptions
and the relation (\ref{number}) from the point of view of probe branes.

\sect{The static probes: thermodynamics}

The D-brane probe is a useful tool to explore the structure of D-brane
bound states (see for example \cite{Dou}-\cite{Youm} and references
therein). Recently the D-brane probes have been used to check a certain
aspect of AdS/CFT correspondence~\cite{AA}-\cite{Cai}. In this section
we consider the static interaction potentials (thermodynamics) of two
kinds of probes in the background of (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states.
One of them is a bound state probe consisting of D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}- and D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes,
or D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe with \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} fields, or noncommutative D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe;
the other is a D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-brane probe.
Let us first discuss the noncommutative D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe.


\subsection{A noncommutative D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe}

Due to the presence of the nonvanishing NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field in the noncommutative
D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe, the probe should have the following action:
\begin{equation}\coord{}\boxEquation{

the occurrence of the NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field in the D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes is always accompanied
by the appearance of D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes in the system, forming (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{})

of D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}- and D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes for the following reasons:

(D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states;
(2) We will see shortly that the static interaction potential of the probe
vanishes in the background of the nonthreshold (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states;
(3) Except for the source \myHighlight{$A^{p}$}\coordHE{} of the D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes, the source \myHighlight{$A^{p-2}$}\coordHE{} of

(4) From its thermodynamics we will obtain further evidence of this
interpretation. Because of the presence of the NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field,
we can also view the probe as a noncommutative D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe.

This implies that from the point of view of the D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-brane probe,
the bound state source (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) can be viewed as
\myHighlight{$N_{p-2}$}\coordHE{} coincident D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes with two smeared coordinates and zero
NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field, while from the noncommutative D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe, the
source is \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{} coincident D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with nonzero NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field. Therefore
from the thermodynamics of probe branes, we again find that the
bound states (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) have two equivalent descriptions.

at the horizon from the point of view of thermodynamics of a probe brane.
Here we provide another evidence to support the argument from the dynamical
aspect of a probe brane.

\sect{Conclusions}

As is well known by now, the worldvolume coordinates of D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes will
become noncommutative if a nonvanishing constant NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is present
on the worldvolume of the D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes. The worldvolume theory is then the
super Yang-Mills theory in a noncommutative space (noncommutative
gauge field theory). Each of the nonthreshold (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states
(\myHighlight{$2 \le p \le 6$}\coordHE{}) can be viewed as a D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane bound state with a
nonvanishing NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field of rank two.

In this paper we have investigated two equivalent descriptions of the
nonthreshold (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound states in the dual gravity description.
In the decoupling limit, the bound states can be described as D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes
with nonvanishing NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field, and then the worldvolume theory is a
noncommutative gauge field with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p+1$}\coordHE{}) dimensions
(with two dimensions compactified on a torus in our case) if the number of
the coincident D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes is \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{}. On the other hand, the nonthreshold
(D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}) bound state will reduce to the solution of D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes
with two smeared coordinates and zero \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field in the decoupling limit and
the large \myHighlight{$au >>1$}\coordHE{} limit. The latter condition is necessary for the validity
of the dual gravity description. The worldvolume theory of the D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes
should be an ordinary gauge field theory with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(N_{p-2})$}\coordHE{} in
(\myHighlight{$p+1)$}\coordHE{} dimensions if the number of the coincident D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes is
\myHighlight{$N_{p-2}$}\coordHE{}. From the viewpoint of the thermodynamics of dual gravity solutions
for the bound states (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}), we have found that \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{} coincident
D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes with NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is equivalent to \myHighlight{$N_{p-2}$}\coordHE{} coincident
D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes with two smeared coordinates and no \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field. In the
equivalence, \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{} and \myHighlight{$N_{p-2}$}\coordHE{} must obey the relation (\ref{number}),
where \myHighlight{$\tilde{V}_2$}\coordHE{} is the area of the two additional dimensions and
\myHighlight{$\tilde{b}$}\coordHE{} is a noncommutativity parameter. When the volume of the torus is
sent to infinity keeping this relation, the ordinary super Yang-Mills theory
reduces to the one with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(\infty)$}\coordHE{} in \myHighlight{$(p-1)$}\coordHE{} dimensions. We
have identified the Yang-Mills coupling constant for the (\myHighlight{$p-1)$}\coordHE{}-dimensional
ordinary Yang-Mills theory.

We have also shown the equivalence from the thermodynamics and dynamics
of two probes in the background of the bound states (D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}, D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}). One
of the probes is a bound state of D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}- and D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-branes, which we called
a noncommutative D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe. The other is a D\myHighlight{$(p-2)$}\coordHE{}-brane probe.
In the asymptotically flat limit, when the two probes fall into the horizon
of the source from spatial infinity, their static interaction potentials
at the horizon  are converted into heat and thereby are absorbed by the
source. In this process, the first law of black hole thermodynamics is
obeyed. In the decoupling limit, we
have found that the thermodynamics and dynamics of the two probes are
identical if the numbers of probe branes satisfy the relation (\ref{3e18}),
completely the same relation as (\ref{number}). As a byproduct, we have
found that the free energy of the noncommutative D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe in the
D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane background with a nonvanishing NS \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field is the same as that
of a D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane probe in the D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-brane background without \myHighlight{$B$}\coordHE{} field. It shows
that the thermodynamics of the noncommutative super Yang-Mills coincides with
the ordinary case in the large \myHighlight{$N$}\coordHE{} limit, not only in the Higgs branch, but
also in the Coulomb branch. In addition, from the analysis of dynamics of
probes, we have derived that the non-extremal D\myHighlight{$p$}\coordHE{}-branes can be located at
the horizon. Our discussions support the argument by Lu and Roy \cite{Lu}
that there is  an equivalence between the noncommutative super
Yang-Mills with gauge group \myHighlight{$U(N_p)$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p+1)$}\coordHE{} dimensions with two dimensions
compactified on a torus and the ordinary one with gauge group
\myHighlight{$U(N_{p-2})$}\coordHE{} in (\myHighlight{$p-1$}\coordHE{}) dimensions when the area of the torus \myHighlight{$\tilde{V}_2
\rightarrow \infty$}\coordHE{}, with the relation (\ref{number}) between \myHighlight{$N_p$}\coordHE{} and
\myHighlight{$N_{p-2}$}\coordHE{}. This result is also consistent with the Morita
equivalence~\cite{Hashimoto2}.
